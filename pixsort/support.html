Frequently Asked Questions

How does PixSort Plus categorize my photos?

PixSort Plus uses Apple's on-device Vision framework to analyze your photos. It detects faces, text, screenshots, and visual content to categorize photos into groups like Selfies, Nature, Food, Documents, and more. All processing happens entirely on your device — no photos are uploaded anywhere.

Where do organized photos go?

When you organize your photos, PixSort Plus creates albums inside an "Organized" folder in your iOS Photos app. Your original photos are not moved or duplicated — they are simply added to the appropriate albums for easy access.

How do I restore my purchases?

Open the app and tap the credit balance badge at the top right. On the purchase screen, tap "Restore Purchases" to restore any previous purchases linked to your Apple ID.

What happens when I delete duplicate photos?

Deleted photos are moved to the "Recently Deleted" album in your iOS Photos app. They remain there for up to 30 days, during which you can recover them. After 30 days, they are permanently deleted by iOS.

Why is the AI categorization not perfect?

AI-based photo analysis provides intelligent suggestions but may occasionally miscategorize photos. You can review and adjust categories before organizing. The categorization improves with clear, well-lit photos.

How do I request a refund?

Refunds are handled by Apple. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com to request a refund for any in-app purchase.

The app is crashing. What should I do?

Try these steps: 1) Make sure you have the latest version of the app, 2) Restart your device, 3) Reinstall the app. If the issue persists, please contact us with your device model and iOS version.

How do I revoke photo access?

Go to your device's Settings > Privacy & Security > Photos > PixSort Plus. You can change the access level or remove access entirely. Note that the app requires photo access to function.
