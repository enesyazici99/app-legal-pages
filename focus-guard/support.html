Frequently Asked Questions

How does FocusGuard work?

FocusGuard locks the apps you choose and gives you a daily walking quest. Complete your step goal to earn temporary access to your blocked apps. Once the unlock timer expires, the apps are locked again until your next quest.

Why does FocusGuard need Screen Time permission?

FocusGuard uses Apple's Family Controls framework to block and unblock your selected apps. This requires Screen Time authorization, which you grant during onboarding. You can revoke this permission at any time in iOS Settings.

Why does FocusGuard need HealthKit access?

FocusGuard reads your step count from Apple HealthKit to track your walking quest progress. Your step data is processed entirely on your device and is never sent to any server.

My step count isn't updating on the shield. What should I do?

The shield screen reads a cached step count that is updated periodically by the main app. Open FocusGuard to refresh your step count, then tap "Check My Steps" on the shield again. Make sure Background App Refresh is enabled for FocusGuard in iOS Settings.

When does my quest reset?

Your daily quest resets at midnight every day. A new quest with your configured step goal is generated automatically. If you completed the previous day's quest, your streak counter increases.

How do I change my step goal or unlock duration?

Go to the Settings tab in the app. You can change your daily step goal (3K, 5K, 8K, or 10K steps) and unlock duration (10, 15, 30, or 60 minutes) at any time.

How do I restore my purchases?

Go to the Settings tab and tap "Restore Purchases". This will restore any previous subscriptions linked to your Apple ID. If you're still having trouble, make sure you're signed in with the same Apple ID you used for the original purchase.

The app is crashing or I found a bug. What should I do?

Try these steps: 1) Make sure you have the latest version of the app, 2) Restart your device, 3) Reinstall the app. If the issue persists, please contact us with your device model, iOS version, and a description of the problem. Screenshots are very helpful!
